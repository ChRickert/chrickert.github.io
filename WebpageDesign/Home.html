Wavefunction methods for solid state matter


December 5th--8th, 2023
My Story Hotel, Gdynia, Poland


Wavefunction methods, including multiconfigurational theory, are well-established techniques for electronic structure calculations of both ground and excited states in chemistry and molecular spectroscopy. However, these methods have been seldom applied in the context of solid-state systems due to their computationally intensive nature and the requirement for additional approximations specifically designed for extended systems. Nevertheless, in recent years, several schemes have been developed that provide opportunities to apply wavefunction methods to solids. The aim of this workshop is to disseminate knowledge about such approaches and promote their understanding.

The Workshop philosophy is based on problem oriented learning.
Main topics to be covered include:
The electronic structure of solids: theory and experiment
Symmetry and basic crystallography
Hartree-Fock/DFT and periodic boundary condition calculations
Electronic correlation in molecules and in solids
Cluster embedding techniques
Relativistic effects and lanthanide chemistry
Properties (electron density, atomic charges, excited states)


Most of the workshop will make use of computers, where simple calculations will be run. Participants must bring their own portable computer. The workshop is intended for a broad audience, including experimentalists. Basic scientific computing skills will be addressed in a dedicated lecture to allow all participants to start with the minimal required preliminary knowledge.

The event contains a poster session and a 'bring your own' project/problem session.

Lectures and computer sessions will be given by prominent scientists and software developers in the field of wavefunction methods for molecules and solids: Jonas Joos (Ghent University), Marek Krośnicki (University of Gdańsk), Denis Usvyat (Humboldt University, Berlin), Valera Veryazov (Lund University).
